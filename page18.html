1221 R.E.M. Accelerate 1 2008 Warner Bros / Wea Rock
 

Accelerate R.E.M.

Release: 2008

Label: Warner Bros / Wea

Genre: Rock

Duration: 34:33

Discs: 1

Producer: Bill Berry, Peter Buck, Jacknife Lee, Mike Mills, Michael Stipe

Date Added: 6 Jan 2017

Summary: Coupla days ago, REM released their new album! Without any fanfare that I noticed. I checked their website as recently as two weeks ago. Out of the blue they suddenly showed up on the Colbert Report! Well, REM is my favorite band, so I was thrilled. I own all of their stuff, even career nadirs like Around the Sun and Up. I was practically holding my breath yesterday, just waiting to get off work. Not only is the sound harder-rockin', it has received some critical acclaim! Not to mention it's currently #1 in Amazon Music! W00t! REM!!! Ahem.

Okay, so what does the CD actually sound like? It's harder rockin,' that's for sure. Not since Monster has REM released such a straight-ahead, pick-to-the-business, fast-paced, rock album. The funny thing is, most of the frills that were woven into Monster are mostly gone. Here and there, some shimmering guitar work, but it mostly just sounds like another Rock Record. I've only listened to it a few times so far, but the album basically sounds very concise. Like, quickly done. Like, accelerated. They've said in the past that Life's Rich Pageant was written in something like two weeks, and I don't have any problem believing similar for this album. But whereas LRP explored various song structures, and weird themes, Accelerate mostly just sounds rockin.' This isn't really bad, but it's somewhat disappointing. Not as disappointing as say, Around the Sun, which had a very snoozy feel.

In comparison to other REM albums, it sort of reminds me of Green. Not that many tracks, couple of obvious singles, here and there some politics. Of course, Green came just before OoT, AftP, M, and NAiHF, a series of true brilliance. I can only hope this is the beginning of another zenith for REM. The other problem with the album is its length. At around 35 minutes, it's among the shortest records they've ever done. Trimmed the fat, obviously. I haven't really looked up from my terminal in a while, but I imagine the music industry has changed a lot in the last 10 years, so maybe this is an effort to appeal to a newer audience. Here's hoping they succeed.
